# 01 — Market Research

## TL;DR

B2B ecommerce is 5x bigger than B2C and growing fast. Shopify is positioning aggressively in B2B but has gated the best features to Plus ($2,300/mo). The gap = SMB stores (Basic/Grow) that want B2B but can't justify Plus — that's our market.

## Shopify ecosystem context

### Plan distribution (estimated)
- **Basic** ($29/mo) — bulk of small DTC stores
- **Grow** ($79/mo) — established small/medium stores
- **Advanced** ($299/mo) — scaling stores
- **Plus** ($2,300/mo) — enterprise

B2B features (Companies, Catalogs per Company, Volume Pricing, Net Terms) are **Plus-only** out of the box. Basic/Grow stores either:
1. Pay $2,300/mo (most can't)
2. Use 3rd-party wholesale apps ($30-100/mo + UX trade-offs)
3. Manage B2B manually (Excel, email, draft orders)

**Our opening:** option 4 — premium-branded app at $39-149/mo that punches above its weight.

## Customer pain points (from Piro Jewelry case + competitor reviews)

### Pain 1 — Existing wholesale apps look generic
- BSS B2B, Bold, B2B Hub all look like Shopify admin (gray Polaris, no customization)
- Premium brands (jewelry, fashion, beauty) don't want their wholesale portal to look "spreadsheet-y"
- Existing apps treat the buyer experience as an afterthought

### Pain 2 — Plus is overkill for small wholesalers
- Brand with 50-200 SKUs and 20 wholesale customers doesn't need enterprise B2B
- $27.6k/year for Plus is not justified by their B2B revenue
- They want a few key features (tiers, quick order, minimum order) without the complexity

### Pain 3 — Manual catalog assignment doesn't scale
- Shopify Plus lets you assign catalogs per Company manually
- After 10+ Companies this becomes unsustainable
- Even on Plus, store owners want **automated** assignment based on customer attributes

### Pain 4 — Generic Shopify errors break the premium experience
- When a wholesaler doesn't meet minimum quantity, Shopify shows a generic gray error page
- This is a brand-breaking moment in the funnel
- No existing app fixes this systematically

### Pain 5 — Mobile experience is poor
- Most wholesale apps were built desktop-first (2018-2022 era)
- 50%+ of B2B browsing now happens on mobile (Forrester)
- Existing apps have unusable tables on mobile

---

## Why now (timing)

1. **Shopify B2B push** — Shopify is aggressively marketing B2B in 2025-2026 (96% YoY GMV growth means demand exists)
2. **AI-augmented dev velocity** — solo developers can ship apps 5x faster than 2-3 years ago
3. **Premium DTC brands going B2B** — Glossier, Diptyque, Aesop archetype = the customer profile growing fastest
4. **Theme App Extensions matured** — only since 2023 has it been possible to ship a polished storefront block without theme edits
5. **App Store still discoverable** — 11,905 apps but B2B category has ~150 (manageable competition)
